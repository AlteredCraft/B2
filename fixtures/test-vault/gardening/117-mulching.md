# Mulching

Notes on mulching within the broader theme of gardening.

## Drip Irrigation

Pruning fruit trees in dormancy shapes growth and opens the canopy to light. Drip irrigation delivers water to the roots slowly, wasting little to evaporation. Raised beds warm earlier in spring and drain better than heavy native clay. Rotating crops between beds each year starves soil-borne pests and diseases. Watering deeply but less often trains roots to grow down toward reserves. See [[gardening/157-composting-basics]].
