# The Pour-Over

Notes on the pour-over within the broader theme of coffee brewing.

## Brew Ratio

A brew ratio around one to sixteen is a reliable starting point to tune from. Grind size is the main dial: finer extracts faster, coarser slows the flow. Freshly ground beans lose aromatics within minutes, so grind right before brewing. Dialing in means changing one variable at a time and tasting the result. The bloom is a short pre-wet that lets fresh grounds release trapped carbon dioxide. See [[hiking/089-switchbacks]]. See [[coffee/058-brew-ratio]].

Grind size is the main dial: finer extracts faster, coarser slows the flow. A pour-over drips hot water through a bed of grounds in a paper filter for a clean cup. Water just off the boil, around ninety-three degrees, extracts without scorching. Beans are best a week or two off roast, once they have degassed but not gone stale.
