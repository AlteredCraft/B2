# The Espresso Shot

Notes on the espresso shot within the broader theme of coffee brewing.

## Grind Size

Grind size is the main dial: finer extracts faster, coarser slows the flow. The bloom is a short pre-wet that lets fresh grounds release trapped carbon dioxide. Water just off the boil, around ninety-three degrees, extracts without scorching. Under-extraction tastes sour and thin; over-extraction tastes bitter and hollow. See [[coffee/028-the-pour-over]]. See [[coffee/098-freshness-and-degassing]].

Beans are best a week or two off roast, once they have degassed but not gone stale. An espresso forces pressurized water through a compact puck for a concentrated shot. A pour-over drips hot water through a bed of grounds in a paper filter for a clean cup. See [[coffee/068-the-bloom]].
