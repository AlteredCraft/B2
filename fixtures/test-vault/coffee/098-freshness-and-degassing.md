# Freshness and Degassing

Notes on freshness and degassing within the broader theme of coffee brewing.

## Freshness and Degassing

Extraction yield is the fraction of the coffee's mass that ends up dissolved in the cup. Under-extraction tastes sour and thin; over-extraction tastes bitter and hollow. The bloom is a short pre-wet that lets fresh grounds release trapped carbon dioxide. See [[distributed-systems/031-the-cap-tradeoff]].

An espresso forces pressurized water through a compact puck for a concentrated shot. Freshly ground beans lose aromatics within minutes, so grind right before brewing. Water just off the boil, around ninety-three degrees, extracts without scorching. Grind size is the main dial: finer extracts faster, coarser slows the flow. Beans are best a week or two off roast, once they have degassed but not gone stale.
