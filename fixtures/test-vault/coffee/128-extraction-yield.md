# Extraction Yield

Notes on extraction yield within the broader theme of coffee brewing.

## Brew Ratio

Dialing in means changing one variable at a time and tasting the result. Water just off the boil, around ninety-three degrees, extracts without scorching. Beans are best a week or two off roast, once they have degassed but not gone stale.

An espresso forces pressurized water through a compact puck for a concentrated shot. A pour-over drips hot water through a bed of grounds in a paper filter for a clean cup. A brew ratio around one to sixteen is a reliable starting point to tune from. Freshly ground beans lose aromatics within minutes, so grind right before brewing. See [[coffee/108-extraction-yield]].
