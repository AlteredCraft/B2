# Brew Ratio

Notes on brew ratio within the broader theme of coffee brewing.

## Tasting Notes

Extraction yield is the fraction of the coffee's mass that ends up dissolved in the cup. Dialing in means changing one variable at a time and tasting the result. Single-origin beans express the character of one farm rather than a blended house style. Under-extraction tastes sour and thin; over-extraction tastes bitter and hollow. Beans are best a week or two off roast, once they have degassed but not gone stale. A brew ratio around one to sixteen is a reliable starting point to tune from.
