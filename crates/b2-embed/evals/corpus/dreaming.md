During REM periods the brain runs vivid, story-like imagery while the body lies
paralyzed. The narratives splice recent events with old memories, which is one
reason researchers tie dreaming to memory consolidation and emotional
processing. Most episodes are forgotten within minutes of waking unless
something interrupts the cycle mid-scene.
