title: Glaciers

---
Rivers of ice that flow downhill a few meters a year, plucking bedrock and
dragging it along as an abrasive underbelly that grinds valleys from a V into a
broad U. The rubble they bulldoze piles into moraines at their edges and end.
Where a glacier retreats faster than snowfall rebuilds it, it leaves behind
lakes, polished rock, and erratic boulders stranded far from home.
