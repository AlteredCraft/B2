The hidden half of a plant: roots anchor the stem against wind and gravity and
draw water and dissolved minerals up from the soil through millions of fine
root hairs. Some species drive one deep taproot toward moisture; grasses spread
a shallow fibrous mat instead. Storage roots like carrots bank sugars
underground for the next season's growth.
