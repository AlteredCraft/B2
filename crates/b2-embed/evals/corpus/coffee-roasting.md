title: Coffee roasting

Raw green beans tumble in a heated rotating drum, drying out, turning yellow,
then browning as sugars caramelize. A few minutes in they audibly pop — the
first crack — the point where a light profile can be stopped; letting the
development run longer darkens the bean, trading origin acidity for bitter,
smoky depth. Overshooting scorches the surface and the batch tastes burnt.
