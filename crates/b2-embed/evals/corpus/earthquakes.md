title: Earthquakes

Stress builds for decades along a locked fault until the rock suddenly slips,
releasing the stored strain as seismic waves that shake the ground for miles.
Magnitude scales run logarithmically, so each whole step is roughly thirty
times the energy of the last. Aftershocks trail the main rupture for days as
the crust settles into its new position.
